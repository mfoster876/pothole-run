# Jamaica Environment & Road-Hazard Research

**Date:** 2026-06-24
**Purpose:** Ground the art, world, and hazard design of *Pothole Run* in the real
beauty, decay, and danger of Jamaican roads and the named locations. Compiled from
parallel web research (Jamaica Gleaner, Observer, JIS, RJR/Radio Jamaica, the Star,
NLJ, ODPEM, UWI, travel and local sources). Sources listed per section.

> Use this as the canon reference when building stage palettes, hazard tables, and
> set dressing. Where a hazard is marked **(documented)** it is grounded in real
> reporting; treat unmarked flavour as plausible extrapolation.

---

## A. Cross-cutting road reality (applies to every stage)

### A1. Potholes — the core motif
Jamaican reporting doesn't say "potholes," it says **"craters."** Recurring Gleaner
letters: "Save us from the craters," "roads riddled with craters." The Star: *"Jamaican
potholes are like craters."* **"Moonscape"** is a literal, repeated trope — cars
"dipping in and out of craters" like a moon rover (good candidate for a bonus-level
register / name).
- Real damage & deaths: sharp-edged craters burst tyres, bend rims; a pothole on the
  Old Harbour main road was tied to a fatal crash. Night driving is worse — craters
  are unlit and unmarked.
- Famous bad corridors: Spanish Town Road, sections of Mandela Highway (wavy/unmarked),
  Junction–Black River, Alexandria–Spaulding, Brown's Town–Falmouth, Portmore schemes.
- Many rural/secondary roads are **marl** (white limestone gravel) or dirt; floodwater
  "eats away the marl under the road," collapsing it into sinkholes. Cheap asphalt
  **patch jobs** sink and re-open — a "repaired" road is a patchwork of mismatched dark
  scars. Hurricane Melissa (Oct 2025) damage assessed ~J$35 billion.

### A2. Stolen / vandalised infrastructure — the signature distinctive mechanic *(documented)*
A genuine, heavily-reported phenomenon: **the road is stripped for scrap metal.**
- Pre-2011-ban scrap theft caused ~**J$1 billion damage in three years** to street
  lights, railway lines, water pipes, bridges, and **road signs**.
- **~36 road signs stolen in Portmore alone, 2012–2014** (NWA). Road Safety Unit
  director Kenute Hare: vandalism is "a serious problem… we have to be continually
  replacing these signs." Specific bare intersections cited (Kingston): East & Charles,
  Church & Charles, Barry & Orange, Beeston & Duke, Pechon & Barry — stop / one-way /
  keep-left signs gone, some reduced to "only a metal remnant" of the post.
- **Guardrails / bridge rails** stripped (e.g. Washington Boulevard). **Manhole and
  water-meter covers** stolen across Kingston, St Andrew, St Thomas, St Catherine —
  leaving open holes in the lane. Replacements bought in scarce foreign exchange, so
  they stay missing a long time.
- Jamaica's scrap-metal "restricted items" list *is* a hazard inventory: manhole
  covers, sign posts, railway lines, copper, irrigation pipes, I-beams, bridges.
- **Visual of a stripped road:** bare metal sign stubs + bolt holes; a stop sign that's
  just an empty pole; a gap in the guardrail over a drop; a black open manhole mid-lane;
  cut street-light poles (explains unlit night levels); coils of stripped cable.

### A3. Livestock & animals *(documented)*
2023: **6 road deaths** from animal collisions (5 stray cattle, 1 dog). A police
constable killed hitting cattle (Mango Hill, Westmoreland); an 11-year-old killed when
a cow ran into the road (St Mary); cattle stray onto Highway 2000. **Goats** are
everywhere (small, constant), **cattle** are the killers (mass), **street dogs** dart
out. Worst at night / on unlit rural roads.

### A4. Road-user chaos — very Jamaican vocabulary *(documented)*
- **"Robot" taxis** — unlicensed cars plying routes; "overtaking like illiterate
  lunatics, creating third lanes at high speed," running lights, tracking police
  checkpoints on WhatsApp.
- **Route taxis ("red plates")** — licensed PPV station-wagons, overstuffed (a 7-seater
  carrying 10), stopping dead anywhere to grab a fare.
- **JUTC buses** — big yellow city buses (flag on the nose) with **conductors** leaning
  out shouting destinations. (Road rage is real: JUTC publicly begged people to "stop
  stoning the buses.")
- **"Undertaking"** (passing on the inside) and "creating a third lane" are named
  behaviours. Vulnerable sharers: **handcart pushers (our protagonist)**, bicycles,
  **"yeng yeng"** motorbike-taxi swarms, pedestrians.

### A5. Makeshift road furniture *(documented)*
- **"Sleeping policemen"** = speed bumps. Residents illegally build their own — random
  heights, unpainted, where you don't expect them (eight unauthorised bumps appeared
  overnight on one Richmond Park road).
- **Improvised hazard markers:** a tree branch or bush jammed in a pothole, a ring of
  rockstones, a breeze-block, an old tyre — because real signs were never there or were
  stolen (A2). *A branch in the road IS the "danger ahead" sign.*
- **Zinc fences** (corrugated sheeting, rusted silver-grey) line roadsides — iconic
  texture; breakdown vehicles park half in the lane with a rock/branch behind them.

### A6. Environmental
Flooding/washouts make roads impassable (280+ roads damaged after Beryl + Melissa);
standing water hides craters. **Bog Walk Gorge / Flat Bridge** (St Catherine) = the
iconic killer corridor (boulders, rockslides, rising river, low bridge) — a perfect
named "gauntlet." Hill fog/mist (Newcastle), **no street lighting at night** (worsened
by stolen light poles), blind walled hairpins.

### Cross-cutting obstacle tiers (quick reference for the spawner)
- **Common:** craters/potholes, goats, DIY speed bumps, rockstone/branch crater-markers,
  route taxis stopping for fares.
- **Mid:** open manholes, missing-guardrail edges, dogs darting out, yeng-yeng swarm,
  breeze-block/breakdown squeeze, JUTC bus.
- **Severe / set-piece:** cattle-herd crossing, flood-hidden craters, Bog Walk boulder
  gorge, fog & unlit-night, blind-corner head-on taxi.
- **Recurring motif / antagonist:** scrap-metal thieves stripping signs, covers,
  guardrails, light poles — the in-world *reason* the road is so deadly.

## B. The named locations

### Fern Gully (Ocho Rios, St. Ann) — MVP stage
- **Beauty:** ~3-mile limestone gorge that swallows the road into a green tunnel; sun
  becomes a dappled glow through 300+ fern species and 30-ft tree ferns arching
  overhead; ~10°F cooler, damp, cathedral-quiet (a dried-up riverbed since the 1907
  earthquake).
- **Horrors:** real fatal-crash history; May 2023 a paint truck overturned and spilled,
  closing the road ~24h; the buried river periodically re-emerges and floods the road;
  poorly lit, perpetually damp, twisty. Heavy trucks banned (signage ignored).
- **Signature look:** arching fern-tree canopy + dappled light shafts; limestone walls
  draped in ferns; roadside craft stalls (carved wooden figures/masks); the
  foliage-camouflaged "Jack in the Bush" stilt-vendor who springs out at buses; cool
  blue-grey shadow palette with sun streaks.
- **Signature hazards:** overturned paint truck + spreading slick; sudden river-flood
  across the road; slick wet shadow patches; vendor stalls pinching the lane; a banned
  truck wedged on a blind bend.

### Holland Bamboo (Lacovia, St. Elizabeth) — MVP stage
- **Beauty:** ~2.5-mile "verdant tunnel" of arching golden bamboo planted in the 1700s;
  said to be the most-photographed spot on the island; soft green archway, sun peeping
  through hurricane-thinned gaps.
- **Horrors:** notorious crash/speeding corridor; repeated school-bus collisions
  (18 students hospitalised, May 2025); Hurricane Melissa snapped the canopy and choked
  it to a single lane, stranding motorists + ~14 ambulances 24h+; flooded by a July 2025
  freak storm. Protected heritage site.
- **Signature look:** continuous golden-bamboo arch with feathery green tops; roadside
  vendors (jelly coconut, spiced peanuts, pepper-shrimp); a yellow children-crossing
  sign near Holland Primary; stray goats/cattle; storm-aftermath layer of snapped bamboo
  + a stranded ambulance line; farm fields at the tunnel mouths.
- **Signature hazards:** goats/cattle crossing; schoolchildren in the school zone;
  speeding oncoming bus on the straight; fallen bamboo culms blocking a lane;
  debris-strewn single-lane pinch; bumpy washboard surface.

### Negril Seven Mile Beach + West End cliffs (Westmoreland) — MVP stage
- **Beauty:** the postcard — miles of white sand, leaning coconut palms, legendary
  sunsets; the West End limestone cliffs over deep blue, cliff-jumping, the white
  lighthouse; laid-back reggae-village colour.
- **Horrors:** **the beach is vanishing** (~1m of sand lost per year; "could be totally
  lost within 30 years"); crumbling infrastructure, dark beachfronts, garbage, relentless
  hustler harassment; **the West End cliff road is the killer** — narrow, twisting, no
  shoulder/sidewalk, fast drivers, "numerous fatalities," a tourist killed by a vehicle
  that lost control at night.
- **Signature look:** leaning palms over a thinning beach; big orange sunset; the
  white cliff lighthouse; Rick's Cafe jumper mid-air; braider/masseur hustlers + craft
  stalls; narrow cliff road, **no guardrail**, sheer blue drop, rasta-painted shopfronts.
- **Signature hazards:** road narrowing where erosion ate the shoulder; **missing
  guardrail** on the cliff edge (drop zone — ties to A2); pothole field on Norman Manley
  Blvd; flooded low section; hustler stepping into the lane; route taxi stopped dead;
  cyclist/pedestrian darting (Three Dives–Rick's); blind cliff curve with oncoming
  speeder; unlit night stretch; stray goat/dog.

### Downtown Kingston + Coronation Market — Phase-2 HARD mode (Tron neon night)
- **Vibrancy:** Coronation Market = "the stomach of Jamaica," the biggest market in the
  English-speaking Caribbean; a feast of stacked yams, jelly coconuts, scotch-bonnets,
  jerk smoke, higglers calling prices in rhyming patter. **Orange Street = "Beat
  Street,"** birthplace of sound-system culture (Prince Buster, Coxsone) — speaker
  stacks, vinyl/CD vendors, reggae from shopfronts.
- **Horrors:** pickpocket/robbery hotspot (worse after dark); flammable stall warrens
  (recurring fires); raw-sewage overflows on Parade/King/Harbour ("Sewage City");
  single-lane roads choked with vendors, refuse, blocked drains.
- **Signature look:** wooden **handcarts** piled high; **zinc fences** + zinc stall
  roofs; hand-built **sound-system speaker boxes**; tarp/umbrella market stalls; produce
  pyramids; **yellow JUTC buses** + red-plate route taxis; cast-iron market hall;
  hand-painted shopfront/record-shop signage; higgler silhouettes.
- **Signature hazards:** **open/missing manhole covers** (stolen — A2); **vanished road
  signs** at junctions → surprise cross-traffic; stolen guardrails near the waterfront;
  handcart cutting across your lane; stall spillover narrowing to one thread; route-taxi
  fare-stop; JUTC bus pulling out; pedestrian surge on market day; sewage/flood puddle
  hiding a pothole; sudden stall fire.

### Montego Bay Pier 1 / Hip Strip (St. James) — Phase-2 stage
- **Beauty:** open-air seafood deck over turquoise harbour; day-to-neon nightclub
  transition; marina masts; calm Marine Park blue; Doctor's Cave umbrellas; warm lit
  waterfront with live music.
- **Horrors:** Pier 1 wrecked twice — **iconic sign destroyed** + building smashed by
  Hurricane Melissa (Oct 2025); Feb 2024 storm "millions in losses." Hip Strip
  harassment peaks 4–7pm (a pitch every ~30s); pickpocket hotspot; drink-driving from
  the club scene.
- **Signature look:** Pier 1 over-water deck + big waterfront sign (intact vs
  storm-snapped); marina sailboats; smoking jerk-pan drum cart; Margaritaville facade +
  Coral Cliff neon; Doctor's Cave umbrellas; tourist-police post + craft stalls.
- **Signature hazards:** storm-surge waves over the deck road; toppled Pier 1 sign;
  flooded street (skid zone); fallen utility pole + downed line across the lane; vendor
  cart into traffic; cruise-crowd swarm; unmarked roundabout; night drunk-driver swerver.

### Old Hope Road (Kingston) — Phase-2 stage
- **Beauty:** Hope Botanical Gardens (largest in the Caribbean, 200 acres) — palm-lined
  avenue, bougainvillea, orchid house; a leafy affluent stretch toward Liguanea.
- **Horrors:** classic Kingston traffic nightmare (rush-hour bottlenecks); a J$1.2B
  water-and-sewer project tore up the corridor into 2026; emblematic **potholes**;
  <10% of roads have drainage → flash flooding.
- **Signature look:** palm-lined Hope Gardens gates amid concrete; pothole-pocked +
  patched asphalt; roadworks kit (cones, trenches, steel plates); route taxis + stuffed
  JUTC buses; flooded sections; mature shade trees.
- **Signature hazards:** pothole field; open road-trench / steel-plate section; flash-
  flood ponding hiding potholes; cone-narrowed lane closures; darting route-taxis;
  pedestrians/vendors at a congested light; stalled bus blocking a lane.

### Portmore (St. Catherine) — Phase-2 stage
- **Beauty:** the **Causeway** over the sea (sunsets over Kingston Harbour, fishing
  boats, skyline across the bay); Hellshire beaches; orderly housing-scheme geometry;
  Dragon City + toll-plaza gateway.
- **Horrors:** **flooding is the signature** (built behind a dyke at/below sea level;
  whole schemes impassable; a drain went two years without clearing → 100+ flooded);
  **storm surge** (~13 ft in Beryl/Melissa); single-corridor isolation risk.
- **Signature look:** narrow causeway ribbon over flat blue sea + Kingston skyline; toll
  plaza booths/barrier arms; tight grids of identical flat-roofed houses with roof water
  tanks + window grilles; **zinc fences**; open concrete gullies beside the road; flood
  water sheeting across a street with a "sleeping policeman" just breaking the surface.
- **Signature hazards:** rising flood water submerging the road (find the crown; water
  hides craters + the gully edge); open-gully fall-in; causeway crosswind/narrow lane +
  roadwork merges; toll-plaza barrier weave; sleeping-policemen jolts; floating debris
  (zinc sheets, branches).

### Norbrook & Cherry Gardens (uptown hills, St. Andrew, Kingston 8) — Phase-2 stage
- **Beauty:** panoramic views over Kingston + harbour (city lights at night); lush
  greenery, tree-lined streets; gated hillside mansions; cool, serene, exclusive.
- **Horrors:** **landslides** (sheared weathered granodiorite; the 1997 Norbrook–Woodford
  debris flow ran ~1km into Sandy Gully; Maryland–Woodford impassable 2026, +30–45 min
  detours); narrow steep winding roads; stark wealth/poverty contrast; fortress
  aesthetic (walls, grilles, electric fences, dogs).
- **Signature look:** gated mansion with spiked/electric-fenced wall + guard hut; burglar
  bars on every window; road hugging the slope (retaining wall uphill, ravine/gully
  downhill, switchbacks); a view-gap revealing Kingston far below; raw-earth landslide
  scar with NWA cones; canopy of palms/mango spilling over walls.
- **Signature hazards:** landslide/debris pile mid-corner (single-lane squeeze);
  crumbling/slumped road edge (breakaway → ravine); blind hairpin with late oncoming
  traffic; **pothole gauntlet even on the "nice" roads** (wealth doesn't buy good roads);
  roadside V-drain fall-in; gate/speed-bump sudden slow; even here, a stolen sign or
  missing cover is true to life.
